Transcription

This page is incomplete and/or missing citations!

There is no universally accepted transcription system for sign languages.

Cross-linguistic

Hamburg Notation System (HamNoSys)

HamNoSys is a system that aims to transcribe all sign languages and was originally developed in 1985 at the University of Hamburg. The latest revision of HamNoSys is HamNoSys 4. (Hanke 2004)

The HamNoSys font is required to display HamNoSys. There is also a LaTeX package on CTAN for HamNoSys.

SignWriting

Sign Language IPA (SLIPA)

SLIPA is a sign language transcription system expressly intended as a sign language analog for the IPA useful for conlanging.

Language-specific

A number of notation systems are specific to certain sign languages.

Stokoe Notation (ASL)

Stokoe Notation transcribes specifically ASL. William Stokoe, its inventor, pioneered sign language linguistics as a whole by analyzing ASL and is in large part responsible for the widespread recognition of sign languages as languages rather than paralinguistic gestures. Being specific to ASL, it only has symbols for the locations, handshapes, and movements used in ASL. (Stokoe 1960)
